Seychelles

Culture

Seychelles is in the Indian Ocean and is made up of 115 islands. It's culture has been affected by the amalgamation of different races- African, European, French and Indian. Moutia is one traditional dance in Seychelles with deep roots in the history of African slavery. It's basically swaying your hips to and fro to the beats of African music. Another traditional dance is Sega, where women dressed in skirts with a wide frill swing their waists.

Music

Seychelles music uses a lot of percussion instruments. Seychellois love playing music in bazaars along the beach. Sega and Moutia is the most popular type of music in Seychelles. Seychelles also has its own version of the European contredanse called the Kontredans, and of Reggae called the Seggae and Mouggae. Zouk is a new genre and is a mix of traditional and modern beats.

Cuisine

The staple food consists of fish and shellfish dishes along with coconut, mangoes and breadfruit. Different types of curry such as shark curry, octopus curry and chicken coconut curry are very popular. Ladob is a traditional dessert consisting of a creamy sauce made up of sweet potato, plantain, coconut milk, nutmeg and vanilla. Takamaka rum is only made in Seychelles and is extremely popular.

Attractions

Creole Festival

The Creole Festival which is held in October every year is a week-long celebration. You can explore cuisines, drinks, music and dance. It takes place on the islands of Praslin, La Digue and Mahe. This festival is a symbolic tribute to the colourful culture, customs, traditions and practices of Creole lifestyle.

Ocean Festival

The Seychelles Ocean Festival is an underwater festival organised every year in December. It is made up of photographic competitions, school events, diving and snorkelling. It is celebrated to remind Seychellois of their underwater reserves and the need to protect them.

Activities to keep you busy

Choose from a rangeof actvities such as hiking, snorkeling, biking on La Digue island and experiencing a glass bottom boat excursion. If you are looking for a more peaceful adventure, try visiting beaches like Beau Vallon, Port Launay and many more.
